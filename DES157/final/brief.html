Final Project Brief

Project Summary 1:

A depression simulation using CSS that offers resources and support at the bottom, and brings the user back out the way they came.

Comparison Project 1: National Alliance on Mental Illness

Nami, the National ALliance on Mental Illness, shares intention with my project idea and has some things that are working and some that could possibly use revision. One of the first things that is nice on arrival to the site is the big directive message to the viewer. I also think their color palette is soothing and fitting for the topic and the typeface is working well too. I think one thing that could possibly be improved is the information heirarchy. Where people can find support is in the middle, and I think having something straight away would be more proompt and perhaps fitting for an individual seeking support or help.

Comparison Project 2: Sgrappa

Sgrappa is another site that shares interest in my topic, in terms of the user interface and the overall flow of interaction. I think there is a lot going on here, but what works really well here is the use of scroll and animation across the z-axis, coming to meet a y-axis and scrolling down the y once it meets a backstop. One thing I think it could improve is perhaps the use of more color? Another thing that is working well here is the pictures positioned in each frame as it scrolls the z-axis, giving it a 3D feel and appearance, despite the images being 2D.

Project Goals:

My goals for the project is to create a simulation of depression for the user via CSS animations through scrolling. It will have a similar trajectory and UI path as the scrappa site, and also have some interaction of messaging using scroll as well. The goal here is to bring the user through a visualization of depression with animation, but then bring them to a place, to a bottom, to a space of arrival where they can find information. I think that is how my project will also connect with the intention of Nami, in that it will be a place that is providing resources and information to those suffering with depression.

Design Strategies:

I want to bring the user upon arrival to the site, into the computer through scroll animations across the z-axis.There will be perhaps an image or some text that the user can see and will scroll until that image is brought into full view on the screen, and as they continue to scroll, it will bring them down, almost as if you were going down a slide, or down an HVAC vent in terms of the architecture. I think the overall color theme for the simulation will be dark with a serif typeface of some kind, so it appears rounded, and softer, rather than rigid and tight. As you scroll down, there will be messages that speak to the user, and will provide a question at the bottom if they want some support or resources. At the bottom, it will give them an option to click, and upon click, will bring them back out of the way they came and to a lighter themed page with resources available to the user.

Interaction Strategies:

I think the biggest strategy for interaction, is going to be the scrolling across the z-axis, along with downward on the y-axis once the image is reached. The main pbjective for this interaction is to simulate depression, bringing someone into the computer, and down a pathway that replicates the experience of depression. I also think the main component of interaction will lie at the bottom of the scroll effect, where the button will be. Upon clicking the button, the interaction will bring the user back out the way they came, reversing the scroll and to another screen, perhaps once the main screen reaches, fading opacity from dark to light and to a page of information.

Experience Goals:

The main overall goals of the experience is two fold: Create a depression simulation through css animations, and create a simulation for coming out of depression towards resources that can help with dealing with this mental illness. I think the overall experience, I really want to bring the user into the screen with the scroll across the z-axis. the idea is to really draw them in, and talk to them along the way through appearing and disappearing text, and in some ways, very similar to what its like being alone, with our thoughts speaking to us, Maybe that can be another part of the experience, where the speaking to the user is sort of a mental simulation of thoughts speaking during the experience.

Audience:

The main target audience is people who are struggling with or have been diagnosed with depression, of any sort. The audience can include individuals who are trying to help those with depression, or who want to learn more about it, but the main target is for people who are currently experiencing depression.
